Korean Pork and Rice Cakes

with Bok Choy

2 serving 15-20 minutes cooktime



Tteokbokki is a classic preparation of Korean rice cakes, a staple of the cuisine.
Our quick-cooking take, made with hearty pork, gets a lift from bok chop, whose
crisp texture perfectly contrasts the tender bite of he rice cakes

Ingredients

  • 10 oz. Ground Pork
  • 1/2lb Rice Cakes
  • 10 oz Baby Bok Choy
  • 2 Tbsps Creme Fraiche
  • 3 Tbsps Savory Black Bean Chili Sauce
  • 1/3 cup Asian-Style Sauteed Aromatics


Preparation

  1. Prepare the bok choy
    • Fill a medium pot 3/4 of the way up with salted water; cover, and heat to boiling on high.
    • Wash, and dry the bok choy; cut off, and discard the root
      ends, then roughly chop.

  2. Brown the Pork
    • In a large pan (nonstick, if you have one), heat a drizzle of olive oil on medium-high until hot.
    • Add the pork; season with salt and pepper. Cook, stirring frequently and breaking the meat apart with a spoon, 4 to 6 minutes, or until browned.
    • Carefully add half the sauteed aromatics(you will have extra). Cook, stirring frequently, 1 to 2 minutes, or until combined.

  3. Cook the bok choy & sauce
    • To the pan, add the chopped bok choy bean-chile sauce (carefully, as the liquid may splatter), and 1 tablespoon of water. Cook, stirring occasionally, 3 to 4 minutes, or until most of the liquid has cooked off and the pork is cooked through.
    • Turn off heat.

  4. Cook the rice cakes
    • Meanwhile, add the rice cakes to the pot of boiling water. Cook 2 to 3 minutes, or until tender.
    • Drain thoroughly.

  5. Finish & serve your dish
    • Add the cooked rice cakes and creme fraiche to the pan of cooked pork and bok choy. Stir to thoroughly combine.
    • Taste, then season with salt and pepper if desired. Enjoy!
